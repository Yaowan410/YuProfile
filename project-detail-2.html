Campus Sustainability Web Platform

Green-Fashion.org empowers students to embrace sustainable fashion through clothing swaps, thrift shop connections, and eco-friendly design.

Background

As part of the Sustainable Fashion Brandeis initiative, I collaborated with four members of the Brandeis Fashion Society to promote sustainability in the fashion industry through mindful consumption and community-driven solutions. Together, we proposed and successfully secured $2,000 in funding to launch a project that combined technology, social engagement, and sustainable design practices.

Development & Technology

I developed green-fashion.org, an online platform that enables secondhand clothing trade among university students. Built with WordPress, the site leverages WooCommerce and Dokan for marketplace functionality, as well as Yoast for SEO optimization.

To improve the user experience, I implemented custom front-end optimizations using HTML5 and CSS, enhancing UI responsiveness and accessibility. Additionally, I designed and integrated a private messaging system, allowing students to connect directly and exchange clothing with ease.

Collaboration & Implementation

The platform was not only supported by the Brandeis Fashion Society but also integrated into campus culture. It was selected as the final project platform for a sociology course at Brandeis University, providing an academic and practical context for exploring sustainable consumption behaviors.

Over 30 students actively used the site during the initial launch, engaging in clothing swaps and discussions around eco-friendly fashion practices.

Impact

Green-Fashion.org bridges technology and sustainability by offering students an accessible and lasting digital resource for secondhand fashion. Beyond facilitating clothing exchanges, it promotes slow fashion, builds community connections, and encourages students to rethink the environmental impact of fast fashion.

The project has demonstrated both social impact and technical achievement, highlighting how digital platforms can empower communities to make more responsible lifestyle choices.
